Act 5 Scene 2
A flash of lightning jolts you from your trance. Looking around you, you see storm clouds brewing.



“Wait, around me?”

You snap back to full alertness, hastily scanning your surroundings and taking stock of your situation. Around you billow dark cumulus clouds, and it is clear that you are standing atop the peak of the mountain. Your heart plunges into a trench of despair.

“It was all a trap. It lulled me into a false sense of security, then possessed me while I was at my weakest and brought me up to the top of the mountain. I should have recognised the signs of hallucination: the dulling of my senses, that tell-tale frangipani scent. I should have been smarter, more perceptive, but I failed.”

Aside from the darkness of the storm clouds, it is clear that noon had turned into evening; the sun is about to set soon. There is no physical way for you to make it down the mountain in time. The vvitch has won. No hallucinations needed; your mind succumbs to the depths of its own despair.


Plumb the depths of your psyche for a glimmer of hope.
