Act 6 Scene 2

The steady beat of helicopter blades float like a cherubim’s song on the wind. The rescue helicopter crests the horizon like a messiah, and tears flood your eyes. You scramble onto the chopper, and as the accursed mountain gets smaller and smaller in the distance you find yourself numb with relief. Shattering your respite, a hauntingly familiar voice echoes in your head, this time tinged with a strange smugness. The pungent smell of helicopter gasoline is impossibly overwhelmed by disgusting sickly sweetness. The dark, brooding clouds twist into the shape of a familiar, repugnant face. Obsidian eyes radiate confidence, boasting of triumph rather than defeat.

“YOU BELIEVE YOU HAVE ESCAPED. LIES, DECEIT. YOU ARE FOREVER BEHELD BY THE ALL-MOTHER.”


As the vvitch's haunting cackles fill your ears, tears of joy give way to heaving sobs.
