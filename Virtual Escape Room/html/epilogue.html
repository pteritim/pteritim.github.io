Epilogue
The sky is a warm ochre. A gentle breeze wafts through the humid air. The stifling heat warms your body and your heart: the familiar sensations of home. Sitting on the couch where you watched Sunday cartoons with your mother, you feel the safety of home enveloping you like a warm blanket. As you gaze at your mother standing in the doorway exchanging final words with the police, you can’t help but sink into the sense of safety and security her familiar presence brings. Mother shuts the door gently as the police quietly depart. She turns to face you with a smile glowing with warmth, her gaze full of motherly love.

“My darling, you must have been so brave. I’m so proud of you. Of course, I always knew you were a fighter. From the moment I first beheld you, I knew you would be special.”

“Beheld…” The phrase hangs in the air like last night's portend moon. A gentle breeze wafts through the apartment. Your mother’s familiar perfume tickles your nose: frangipani in full bloom. As your eyebrows betray the slightest uneasy crease, her jet-black irises stare at you piercingly.
